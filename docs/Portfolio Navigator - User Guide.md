# Portfolio Navigator - User Guide

Portfolio Navigator helps managers do three practical things:

-   Quickly approve monthly allocation snapshots for their team
-   Adjust assignments when an employee's work changes
-   Review the Services & Initiatives hierarchy when more context is needed


# Your role as a manager in the app

Most months, the main job is simple:

1.  open the current month's snapshots
2.  review your direct employees
3.  approve the ones that still look correct
4.  make assignment changes only when someone's work has changed

If nothing has changed for an employee since last month, you will usually spend most of your time in the Snapshots tab.

>If someone's work has changed, you will usually update their Assignments first, then return to Snapshots to review or approve the monthly record.

# Important Terms

### Assignments

Assignments are the standing records that describe what an employee is working on.

Think of them as the current plan for that employee's work.

### Snapshots

Snapshots are the monthly records that managers review and approve.

They reflect a specific period and are the main thing you approve each month.

### Services & Initiatives

These are the work items employees are assigned to.

You can browse them by hierarchy or search for them by name and other metadata.

### My Direct Team

This is your root manager view in the app.

You can drill into subordinate managers below you, but the app stays within your hierarchy.

### Training Mode

Training Mode uses safe demo data.

If you are practicing or showing the app to someone new, use Training Mode. If you are doing your real monthly work, stay in live data.


# The Main Tabs

### Snapshots

Use this for monthly review and approval.

This is where most managers will spend most of their time each month.

### Assignments

Use this when an employee's work has changed and their standing assignment mix needs to be updated.

### Services & Initiatives

Use this when you need to understand the hierarchy, find the right service, or look up ownership and leadership details.

# The Typical Monthly Workflow

For most managers, the normal monthly workflow looks like this:

1.  Open the Snapshots tab.
2.  Stay in My Direct Team unless you need to drill into a manager below you.
3.  Review employees whose work has not changed.
4.  Approve those snapshots one at a time or in bulk.
5.  If something changed for an employee, switch to Assignments, update their standing work, then return to Snapshots to review again.

That is the core experience the app is designed to support.

# Approving Snapshots That Have Not Changed

>This is the most common monthly task.

## What to Look For

On the Snapshots tab, each employee row shows:

-   the employee name
-   their service and initiative chips for the selected period
-   the next available action, such as Ready, Approved, Review, or Import

>If an employee's snapshot still looks correct and totals 100%, you can approve it directly.

## Approving One Employee

1.  Open the Snapshots tab.
2.  Confirm the correct period is selected.
3.  Review the employee's row.
4.  If everything looks right, click Ready.

## Approving Several Employees

1.  Use the checkboxes beside employees who are ready.
2.  Click Approve Selected.
3.  Confirm the action.

>If everyone in view is ready, you can also use Approve All.

## If You Manage Other Managers

If a direct report is also a manager, click their row in the Direct Reports section to drill down.

Use the Up control before the Manager label to move back up one level.

# When Work Has Changed

If an employee's work has changed, update the standing assignments first.

That keeps the long-term record correct and helps future snapshots stay aligned.

## Basic Assignment Update Flow

1.  Open the Assignments tab.
2.  Find the employee.
3.  Open their assignment editor.
4.  Add, remove, or adjust services and percentages.
5.  Save the changes.
6.  Make sure the employee totals 100%.

If an employee is under- or over-allocated, the app will warn you.

> The Issues filter is a quick way to find employees whose assignments do not total 100%.

## When to Come Back to Snapshots

After updating assignments, return to Snapshots to review the current month's record.

If the monthly record needs adjustment too, open the employee in Snapshots and save or approve from there.

# Reviewing the Services & Initiatives Hierarchy

Use the Services & Initiatives tab when you need context.

This tab is useful when:

-   you are trying to find the right service to assign
-   you want to understand where a service sits in the hierarchy
-   you need to review ownership, lead, or descriptive information

## What You Can Search By

In the main Services & Initiatives tab, you can search by:

-   service or initiative name
-   DRI or owner
-   lead names
-   description text
-   other keywords related to the service

> If you cannot find something in the smaller assignment or snapshot picker, use this tab for a broader search experience.

## What You Will See

Service cards may show:

-   the service name
-   parent relationship
-   description
-   owner or DRI
-   PM lead or engineering lead
-   other leadership details when available

# A Few Helpful Tips

-   Start in Snapshots each month unless you already know assignments changed.
-   Use Assignments to fix the standing plan, not just the monthly symptom.
-   Use My Direct Team as your main landing point.
-   Drill into subordinate managers only when needed.
-   Use the main Services & Initiatives tab when a lookup search is too narrow.
-   If you are learning the app, use Training Mode first.

# FAQ

## Why can't I approve someone?

Usually because the employee's total does not equal 100% for that period. Review the row or open the employee to correct it.

## Should I update Snapshots or Assignments?

If the employee's work has actually changed, update Assignments first.

Use Snapshots for the monthly review and approval step.

## Will Training Mode affect real data?

No. Training Mode uses demo data only.
